What is Christmas

Christmas is a Christian holiday that celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ. It is celebrated on December 25th every year by millions of people around the world.

History of Christmas

Originates from the story of Jesus’s birth in BethlehemBecame a public holiday in the Roman Empire in the 4th centuryCombines Christian and pagan traditions (e.g. gift-giving, feasts)

What people usually do on Christmas Day

On Christmas Day, people around the world engage in a variety of joyful activities that bring families and communities together. Many people begin the day by exchanging gifts with loved ones, a tradition that symbolizes generosity, love, and the sharing spirit of the holiday. Children often wake up early, excited to see what Santa Claus has left for them in their stockings or under the Christmas tree. Families gather in the living room, where presents are unwrapped, laughter fills the air, and heartfelt moments are shared.After the gift exchange, many families prepare a special Christmas meal. This meal often includes traditional dishes such as roasted turkey, ham, mashed potatoes, gravy, vegetables, and desserts like fruitcake or Christmas pudding. In some countries, people enjoy unique holiday foods based on local culture and customs. The Christmas dinner is a time for family members to sit down together, give thanks, and enjoy each other’s company in a warm and festive setting.
